# Journal — the human story behind the bot

This folder holds the **why** behind the **what** in `ROADMAP.md` and `specs/`. Where those documents tell you how the software works, this folder tells you why this particular software exists, what kind of business it is meant to serve, and what kind of person it is meant to free.

## Who this is for

This journal is written for three audiences, in layers:

1. **The author's future self** — to come back in a year or five and remember why decisions were made. Reflective. Personal where it earns the right to be.
2. **A successor** — a family member, a partner, an inheritor of this business who needs to understand not just the operations but the principles. Teaching tone where it matters.
3. **A thoughtful stranger** — a researcher, a future contributor, a small-business owner walking a similar path. Professional but human.

Most paragraphs serve all three audiences at once. Where one paragraph is clearly for one audience, it says so.

## How to read this folder

| File | What it is | When to read |
|---|---|---|
| `README.md` | This file. Map to the rest. | First. |
| `philosophy.md` | The timeless principles. No dates. The beliefs underneath the business. | When you want to remember *why*. |
| `people.md` | How we think about the people who work in this business — the categories of helpers, the role-shapes that recur. | When deciding who does what, or hiring. |
| `decisions.md` | Big business-flavored decisions: what we chose, what we rejected, and the human reasoning behind both. | When a future call feels familiar — check whether we've already thought about it. |
| `sessions/` | Chronological long-form summaries of conversations between John (owner) and his AI thinking-partner. Append-only. | When you want the unfiltered story of how an idea evolved. |

## A note on tone

This is a private repository. The author's team members are mentioned by name where it strengthens the record. The tone aims to be respectful enough that any person mentioned here could read this folder and feel seen rather than surveilled. If this repository is ever opened to a broader audience, the names get anonymized in a sweep pass before publication.

## A note on permanence

Git is forever. Every word committed here lives in the repository's history. That is why the writing aims for principles over gossip, patterns over personalities, and the long view over the moment's frustration. If a sentence here would embarrass the author or hurt a team member five years from now, it does not belong.

## A note on the format

The structure of this folder mirrors how serious operators think — separating what is *true always* (philosophy) from what is *true about my team* (people) from what *we decided* (decisions) from what *happened* (sessions). When updating, always ask: which of these four kinds of content is this? Then write it there.
